### **Amateur Bandit**

- **Category:** Humanoid, Common Threat
- **Description:** Low-level brigands, often found in groups preying on the weak or unguarded travelers. They lack training and rely on numbers or intimidation to overwhelm targets. Easily rattled when faced with unexpected resistance or the loss of their leader.

---

#### **Stats**
- **Health (HP):** 10
- **Base Damage (Weapons):**
  - Sword: 3
  - Dagger: 2
- **Evasion (Evade):** 8 (reduced by 2 when panicked or distracted)
- **Agility:** 10 (Below-average adult human)
- **Speed:** 10 (Below-average adult human; 12–20 km/h sprinting)
- **Armor:** None (0 protection)

---

#### **Combat Traits**
- **Skill Level:** Basic (untrained in advanced tactics or techniques)
- **Morale:** Low (quickly shaken by casualties or surprises)
- **Tactics:** Favor simple ambushes or overwhelming numbers but lack discipline in prolonged combat.
- **Leadership Dependency:** Bandits heavily rely on a leader to direct their actions. Loss of leadership often results in panic or retreat.

---

#### **Behavior**
- **Motivation:** Driven by greed or survival; their main goal is to steal valuables with minimal resistance.
- **Flight Risk:** High. When overwhelmed or outnumbered, they will flee without hesitation.
- **Interaction:** Prone to bluffing or intimidation but will surrender if faced with superior force.

### **Amateur Bandit Leader**
- **Category:** Humanoid, Minor Threat
- **Description:** The slightly more experienced leader of a bandit group. Often chosen for their size, strength, or charisma rather than true skill, these leaders maintain control over their crew through intimidation or cunning. They are more determined than their followers but are still outmatched by trained fighters.

---

#### **Stats**
- **Health (HP):** 15
- **Base Damage (Weapons):**
  - Sword: 4
  - Warhammer or Mace (if applicable): 6
- **Evasion (Evade):** 9
- **Agility:** 15 (average adult human)
- **Speed:** 10 (Same as bandits, slightly slower reactions but able to keep up with their group)
- **Armor:** Light armor or padded leather (1 Damage Reduction)

---

#### **Combat Traits**
- **Skill Level:** Moderate (competent in basic combat tactics but lacking finesse)
- **Morale:** Medium-High (Leaders are less likely to flee but may retreat strategically if outnumbered)
- **Tactics:**
  - Favors brute force or intimidation.
  - Will often focus on disrupting the strongest foe while letting the weaker bandits harass the rest.
  - Slightly more disciplined than their crew, but still relies on numbers.

---

#### **Behavior**
- **Motivation:** Greedy and ambitious, often using the bandit crew as a means to personal gain.
- **Leadership:** The linchpin of a bandit group’s morale and coordination. If the leader falls, the group will likely scatter.
- **Interaction:** May negotiate or bluff if cornered, but prone to anger and desperation if things go wrong.

---

#### **Special Notes**
- **Bandit Crew Dependency:** A leader’s effectiveness increases significantly when backed by a crew. Alone, they are far less dangerous.
- **Combat Focus:** Bandit leaders often target the strongest or most vocal opponent first, trying to break the group’s resolve.

### **Bestiary Entry: Korric**

---

#### **Name:** Korric  
#### **Role:** Veteran Warrior, Ally to Mizhael  
#### **Faction:** Sundered Legion  
#### **Appearance:**  
Korric is a rugged and battle-hardened warrior. His muscular frame is scarred from countless battles, and his dark, close-cropped hair reveals streaks of gray. His sharp, piercing eyes constantly scan his surroundings with the vigilance of a seasoned fighter. He wields a well-worn but deadly two-handed axe, a weapon that’s seen as much combat as its owner.  

---

#### **Combat Stats:**
- **Constitution:** 30  
- **Willpower:** 10
- **Agility:** 20 (Moderate agility for a melee fighter).
- **Accuracy:** 20
- **Evade:** 20
- **Attack:**  
  - **Axe Swing:** Deals **10 damage** with standard melee rolls.  
  - **Cleave:** Hits multiple enemies in range for **8 damage each** (requires 5 Stamina).  
- **Defense:**  
  - **Parry:** Reduces incoming melee damage by 5 when prepared.  
 

---

#### **Skills and Traits:**
1. **Veteran Tactician:** Years of battle experience allow Korric to anticipate enemy moves and act decisively.  
2. **Relentless Fighter:** Korric thrives in melee combat, utilizing brute force and endurance to outlast opponents.  
3. **Steady Presence:** Korric provides morale and guidance to allies, offering tactical advice during battles and missions.  

---

#### **Behavior:**
- **Tactical:** Korric prefers direct, strategic combat, assessing his opponent's strengths and weaknesses before engaging.  
- **Protective:** Loyal to his allies, he prioritizes their survival while maintaining his own effectiveness in battle.  
- **Blunt but Wise:** His no-nonsense attitude often hides a wealth of practical knowledge and wisdom.

### Bestiary Entry: Grey Rooks' Caravan Guards

---

#### **Grey Rooks’ Caravan Guard**
- **Category:** Humanoid, Common Threat
- **Description:** Basic infantry in the Grey Rooks' ranks, these guards are hired to protect valuable caravans and supply routes. While not highly trained, their numbers and equipment make them a credible threat. They rely on coordination and brute force over finesse, often outmatched by experienced fighters.

---

### Stats:
- **Health (HP):** 10
- **Base Damage (Weapons):**
  - Sword: 4
  - Bow: 3 (if equipped for ranged combat)
- **Evasion (Evade):** 8
- **Agility:** 8
- **Speed:** 10
- **Armor:** Light armor (1 Damage Reduction)

---

### Combat Traits:
- **Skill Level:** Basic (trained for rudimentary guard duties, but unskilled in advanced tactics).
- **Morale:** Medium (hold their ground until the odds turn against them; panic if outnumbered or outmatched).
- **Tactics:** Guards maintain formation around the caravan and prioritize protecting high-value assets. They will engage aggressively if they sense a weak point in the attackers' strategy.

---

### Behavior:
- **Motivation:** Guarding caravans for pay, with loyalty dependent on the promise of rewards or fear of their employers.
- **Flight Risk:** Moderate. Guards may abandon the fight if the caravan appears lost or if their leader is taken out.
- **Interaction:** Professional and detached. May negotiate if vastly outnumbered or outmaneuvered.

---

### Special Notes:
- **Weaknesses:**
  - Light armor offers minimal protection against precise or powerful attacks.
  - Poor adaptability in dynamic combat situations.
- **Strengths:**
  - Effective in groups, where their coordination and positioning create a significant obstacle.
  - Dependable for holding defensive positions when commanded by a leader.
